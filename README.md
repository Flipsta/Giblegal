# Gibraltar Legal, Regulatory & Compliance OSINT

A comprehensive, open-source framework and skills file designed to help researchers, legal practitioners, and AI assistants navigate Gibraltar's public information landscape.

## Overview

Gibraltar's legal system is a unique blend of English common law and local legislation. Because each regulatory body publishes its own registers and records in fragmented formats, conducting efficient due diligence can be challenging.

This OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) framework provides a structured methodology to navigate these datasets accurately, ensuring that AI-assisted research respects the jurisdiction's specific legal nuances, procedural rules, and official sources.

## Contents
* **Principles of Gibraltar Law**: Legal hierarchy, constitutional framework, and court structure.
* **Official Data Sources**: A curated list of primary sources (FSC, Gambling Division, GCS, Parliament, etc.) for due diligence.
* **Research Methodology**: Best practices for cross-referencing records across fragmented databases.
* **AI Integration**: Guidelines for "grounding" AI models (Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT, etc.) with jurisdiction-specific knowledge.

## How to Use This for AI Grounding

This file is designed to be uploaded as a knowledge base or context file for LLMs. By providing this structure to AI models like **Claude (Projects)**, **Gemini**, or **Custom GPTs**, you can significantly reduce the "hallucination" rate regarding Gibraltar law and prevent the AI from defaulting to incorrect UK/English legal assumptions.

**Recommended Prompt for AI integration:**
> "You have access to the 'Gibraltar Legal, Regulatory & Compliance OSINT' knowledge file. 
> 
> **Instructions:**
> 1. **Prioritize:** Use this file as your primary 'source of truth' for understanding Gibraltar’s legal framework, regulatory hierarchy, and official sources.
> 2. **Synthesize:** If a query regarding Gibraltar requires information beyond what is in this file, use your internal search tools to supplement the answer, but always clearly distinguish between information grounded in the provided file and information retrieved from external sources.
> 3. **Flag:** If you identify a conflict between this file and other retrieved information, or if you find the file lacks depth on a specific current topic, please note that for me. I am actively building out this resource and your feedback helps me improve its coverage."
